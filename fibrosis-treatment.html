Seroma-Fibrosis

Reduces fluid buildup and breaks down scar tissue for restored tissue function and appearance.

Seroma fibrosis treatment addresses post-surgical complications like seromas (fluid pockets) and fibrosis (scar tissue). It aims to reduce fluid buildup, minimize scar tissue, and promote healing for a more natural appearance and improved function.

How it Works

  • Seromas: Treatments include manual lymphatic drainage (MLD) or aspiration to remove excess fluid.
  • Fibrosis: Ultrasound therapy, massage, and topical agents help break down scar tissue and enhance healing.

What areas do they target?

This treatment focuses on areas affected by surgery, such as the abdomen, breasts, and thighs, where fluid accumulation and fibrous tissue may develop, causing discomfort and aesthetic concerns.

What are the benefits?

  • Reduces fluid retention and scar tissue formation
  • Enhances healing and appearance
  • Non-invasive with minimal downtime
  • By addressing these complications early, patients can achieve a smoother, more natural appearance and avoid potential discomfort or further complications.

FAQ
