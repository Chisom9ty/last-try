Lemonbottle Treatment

Compound breaks down stubborn fat cell membranes resistant to exercise.

Lemon Bottle treatment uses deoxycholic acid to target and break down fat cells that are resistant to diet and exercise. This acid disrupts fat cell membranes, causing them to disintegrate and be naturally expelled by the lymphatic system. This treatment is effective for reducing fat in areas like the double chin or jawline and is ideal for those close to their ideal weight but struggling with stubborn fat pockets.

What is in Lemon Bottle Solutions

The solutions are composed of plant-based compounds refined in a lab. These ingredients disrupt fat cell membranes, leading to fat breakdown and elimination by the body's immune system and liver.

Ingredients in Lemon Bottle Injections

  1. Deoxycholic Acid
  2. Vitamin B-12
  3. Vitamin B-6
  4. Vitamin B Complex
  5. Branched Chain Amino Acids (BCAAs)
  6. L-carnitine
  7. MIC (Methionine, Inositol, Choline)

Is this Treatment for me

This treatment is best for individuals who are close to their ideal weight but have localized areas of stubborn fat despite maintaining a healthy lifestyle.

What areas do they target?

  • Double chin
  • Moobs
  • Bra and underarm pouches
  • Upper arms
  • Saddlebags
  • Inner thighs
  • Abdomen (including six-pack definition)

What to expect on the day of your treatment

  • Consultation: Discuss your suitability for the treatment. Treatment can be performed the same day or scheduled for a later date.
  • Treatment: The area is marked, and the solution is injected. The treated area may be massaged, and you will need to continue this at home.
  • Post-Treatment: Expect swelling, discoloration, and tenderness for 7-10 days. Results are typically visible after 1-2 treatments, with up to 8 sessions

FAQ

• Under 18 years old • Active infection near injection site (e.g., cold sores) • Allergy/hypersensitivity to Aqualyx • Pregnant or breastfeeding • Abnormal bleeding disorders (e.g., haemophilia, thrombocytopenia) • Open wounds, scabs, bleeding, blisters • Skin conditions like rashes, psoriasis, eczema, rosacea (pustular or nodular), fungal infections • Contagious skin conditions • Raised moles, warts, changing moles, unidentified skin growths • Bruised skin • Skin cancer • Keloid scars • Anti-coagulant medications • Medications causing photosensitivity • Areas with topical medications • Recent cosmetic treatments (micro-dermabrasion, IPL, chemical peels, surgery) • Diabetes • Liver or kidney disease • Thyroid issues (consult GP)

After care

  • Avoid high temperature stimulation (sauna, steam room, hot tub, hot packs) for two weeks.
  • Cleansing, makeup, light showers are okay on the day of injection.
  • Avoid exercise, excessive sweating, and sport massage.
  • Avoid touching or pressing the treated area excessively.
  • No drinking or excessive smoking.
  • Maintain a balanced, healthy diet for optimal results.
  • Drink at least 0.5 litres of water immediately post-treatment, then 2 liters daily for one week.
